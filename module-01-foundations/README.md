# Module 01 - Foundations & History
 
> An in-depth look at the origin of the XRP Ledger, the engineers behind it, the timeline of key milestones, and the critical distinctions between XRP, XRPL, Ripple, and the XRPL Foundation.
 
 
## The Origin: "Bitcoin Without Mining"
 
In 2011, three engineers, David Schwartz, Jed McCaleb, and Arthur Britto, were fascinated with Bitcoin but identified a fundamental problem: the enormous energy waste produced by mining.
 
In a May 2011 forum post titled "Bitcoin without mining," McCaleb started the conversation that would lead to the birth of the XRP Ledger. The core idea was to build a system for sending value quickly, cheaply, and sustainably, without the need for miners.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history)
 
 
## Timeline
 
### 2011 - Development Begins
 
In early 2011, David Schwartz, Jed McCaleb, and Arthur Britto began developing the XRP Ledger. Their goal was to create a more sustainable system for sending value, one that improved upon Bitcoin's core limitations: high energy consumption, scalability constraints, and centralization risks from mining pool consolidation.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history)
 
### 2012 - The Ledger Launches
 
By June 2012, Schwartz, McCaleb, and Britto finished code development and the Ledger went live. Shortly thereafter, they were joined by Chris Larsen, and the group started a company called NewCoin in September 2012, quickly renamed OpenCoin, and now known as Ripple.
 
The XRPL founders gifted 80 billion XRP, the platform's native currency, to the company. Ripple has since put the majority in escrow.
 
The codebase was originally called "Ripple," and the digital asset was originally called "ripples" with the currency code XRP, following the same naming convention as Bitcoin. Over time, the community started referring to the digital asset simply by its currency code: XRP.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history)
 
### 2013 - Rebranding to Ripple Labs
 
With the community referring to the digital asset as XRP, company leaders decided to rebrand from OpenCoin to Ripple Labs, later shortened to Ripple. This helped clarify the distinction between the open-source ledger and the private company building products on top of it.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history)
 
### 2020 - XRPL Foundation Launched
 
Founded on September 24, 2020, the XRPL Foundation is an independent, nonprofit entity with a mission to accelerate the development and adoption of the decentralized XRP Ledger. Its mission is to ensure the XRP Ledger remains open, transparent, and accessible to all.
 
The Foundation received an initial donation of over $6.5M from Coil, Ripple, and Gatehub to fund its work in service of the growing global developer community.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history), [xrpl.foundation](https://xrpl.foundation)

| Founder | Role |

| **Jed McCaleb** | Co-founder and CTO. Initiated the original forum discussion in 2011. Later founded Stellar in 2014. |

Source: [xrpl.org/about/history](https://xrpl.org/about/history)
 
 
## Critical Distinction: XRP vs XRPL vs Ripple vs XRPLF
 
This is one of the most common points of confusion in the ecosystem. The following definitions reflect the official positions of each entity:
 
| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **XRP** | The native digital asset of the XRP Ledger |
| **XRPL** | The XRP Ledger, the open-source decentralized network |
| **Ripple** | A private company that builds payment products using XRPL. It does not own or control the network. |
| **XRPLF** | The XRP Ledger Foundation, a nonprofit supporting the open-source ecosystem |
 
The XRP Ledger is a decentralized, public blockchain. Any changes that would impact transaction processing or consensus need to be approved by at least 80% of the network. Ripple is a contributor to the network, but its rights are the same as those of other contributors. In terms of validation, there are 150+ validators on the network, and Ripple runs only one of them.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about](https://xrpl.org/about), [xrpl.org/about/faq](https://xrpl.org/about/faq)
 
 
## The Original Mission
 
The XRP Ledger is a decentralized public blockchain built for business. The peer-to-peer network that manages the ledger is open to everyone. It is maintained by software engineers, server operators, users, and businesses, a global community working to solve problems and create real-world value.
 
The goal from the start was not to replace the existing financial system, but to improve it. This philosophy is reflected in every technical decision in the protocol.
 
Source: [xrpl.org/about](https://xrpl.org/about)
 
 
## Key Technical Facts (Quick Reference)
 
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Development started | 2011 |
| Network launched | June 2012 |
| Core implementation | rippled (C++) |
| License | ISC License (open source) |
| Total XRP supply | 100,000,000,000 (fixed, pre-mined at genesis) |
| Transaction finality | 3-5 seconds |
| Throughput | ~1,500 TPS (sustained) |
| Minimum fee | 10 drops (0.00001 XRP) |
| Mining | None |
